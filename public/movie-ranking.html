The Best Movies

My top 3 movies of all-time.

Despicable Me 3

Despicable Me 3 is a colorful and entertaining animated movie known for its vibrant animation style, humorous storytelling, and strong family themes. The film follows Gru, his wife Lucy, their adopted daughters, and the lovable Minions as they face a new villain, Balthazar Bratt, a former child star obsessed with the 1980s. The animation is exaggerated and cartoonish, filled with energetic action scenes and slapstick comedy, especially from the mischievous Minions. The movie explores emotional themes like family bonds, sibling rivalry, and personal growth when Gru discovers he has a cheerful and wealthy twin brother named Dru. Music plays a major role in the film, with Bratt using 80s pop songs as part of his villainous acts, adding a fun retro vibe. Overall, the movie balances comedy, action, and emotion, making it enjoyable for both children and adults.

Lucky Bhashkar

Lucky Bhaskar is a movie completely based on the world of shares and the stock market. The story revolves around a common man named Bhaskar, who rises from a simple middle-class life to achieve big success through his knowledge and involvement in the stock market. The film highlights the ups and downs of the financial world, the risks involved in stock trading, and how sudden success can also bring challenges and danger. Cinderella

Cinderella is a timeless fairy tale movie known for its magical, emotional, and inspirational storytelling. The story follows a kind-hearted young girl named Cinderella who, despite being mistreated by her stepmother and stepsisters, remains gentle and hopeful. The film emphasizes values like kindness, patience, and inner strength. With the help of her Fairy Godmother, Cinderella's life transforms magically as she gets the chance to attend the royal ball, where she meets the prince. The movie features beautiful visuals, elegant costumes, and enchanting music that create a dreamy, fantasy-like atmosphere. It highlights the idea that goodness and hope can lead to a better future, making it a beloved tale for both children and adults. The themes of love, transformation, and justice are central to the story, giving it a universal appeal across generations.
